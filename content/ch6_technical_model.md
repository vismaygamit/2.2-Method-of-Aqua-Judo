
## TECHNICAL MODEL
**The Wave**
*Timing geometry for reading and responding to instability*

Every instance of balance disruption follows a wave structure. Whether the source is a surf swell, a partner’s pull, a stumble on uneven ground, or the onset of fatigue—the geometry is the same.

The practitioner who understands this geometry does not react to instability. They read it. And a body that reads instability before it arrives is a body that does not fall.

### THE FOUR PHASES
* **Collapse** — The wave descends. Structure is disrupted. This is the moment of vulnerability—and, for a trained practitioner, the moment of entry.
* **Pause** — The wave reaches its lowest point. A brief stillness. The nervous system recalibrates. In partner work, this is the entry window.
* **Recovery** — Balance is being restored. The window is closing.
* **Intercept** — At approximately two-thirds of recovery, mechanical advantage peaks. This is where the throw lives—and where the trained body instinctively moves.
 
A body that has internalized this wave does not need to think about balance. It feels the wave. It moves with it. This is what ten years of judo builds. Aqua Judo builds it faster.
