
## DAILY PRACTICE
**The Training Rhythm**
*How every session is structured*

Three phases. Every session. No exceptions.

### ACTIVATION
Sensory recalibration. Three-breath reset. Slow, deliberate movement to reestablish Shisei. The nervous system is signalled: the predictable world is behind you. What comes next requires your full attention.

### EXPLORATION
The session ends when movement quality degrades—not at a fixed time. You are hunting efficiency, testing balance, reading the terrain. No sets. No reps. A continuous dialogue between your structure and the environment.

### REGULATION
Down-regulation through rhythmic breath and gentle mobility. This is where the learning consolidates. Without this phase, the session’s gains remain fragile. The body adapts during recovery, not during effort.

The nervous system does not count reps. It counts quality. The session ends when quality degrades. Continuing past that point teaches the degraded pattern, not the refined one.
