
## APPLICATIONS
**The Seven Days**
*Cabo Bello • Cabo San Lucas, Mexico*

Seven days. Three people. One coast. The method does not change across the week—what changes is the depth of the problem. Each day adds a layer of complexity until, by day four, there are no more drills. Only movement.

*   **LOCATION**: Cabo Bello
*   **DURATION**: 7 Days
*   **GROUP**: 3 People
*   **FORMAT**: Residency

### DAY 1: The Grounding
Sand dunes of Cabo Bello. Tsugi-ashi at walking pace. The three-breath structural reset becomes the session baseline. Everything slow, everything honest. The body begins to remember what ground actually feels like.
*OBJECTIVE: Reestablish the sensory connection between the feet and the nervous system.*

### DAY 2: Reading the Ground
Irregular coastal rock. Active Recon scanning. Variable elevation steps. The eye learns to commit five meters ahead of the foot. The nervous system begins to understand it is being given new problems—and that it already has the tools to solve them.
*OBJECTIVE: Train the eye to lead the body.*

### DAY 3: The Water
Fluid resistance drills, breath-sync floating, hydrostatic core work. CO₂ exposure begins—controlled, gradual, always safe. The surf does not accommodate errors. The Blue Mind effect begins.
*OBJECTIVE: Build the breath cycle under real environmental pressure.*

### DAY 4: The Pathfinding
Sand to rock to surf and back. No drills. No structure beyond the method itself. The practitioner navigates in real time. This is what the first three days built toward. The body is pathfinding now.
*OBJECTIVE: Move through the coast as a continuous environment.*

### DAY 5: Dynamic Integration
Lateral movement, Tai-sabaki under load, Tobi Komi entries on sand. Full breath cycle at full pace. The most technically demanding session of the week—and the one where the week’s investment becomes visible.
*OBJECTIVE: Apply the full method at speed across all three environments.*

### DAY 6: The Endurance of Calm
Sustained sequences until mild fatigue sets in. The breath shortens first. The alignment drifts second. The session is the practice of catching both before they compound. This is what resilience training looks like.
*OBJECTIVE: Sustain quality under accumulated fatigue.*

### DAY 7: The Synthesis
A quiet revisit of each environment—not to perform, but to observe. The sand is lighter. The water is familiar. The rock is a path. That shift is the method, embodied. Carry it home.
*OBJECTIVE: Let the week settle into a new movement baseline.*

Bring what you can move in. Leave what needs to be performed.
